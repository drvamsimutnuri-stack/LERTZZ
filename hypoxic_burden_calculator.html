What is hypoxic burden (HB)?

HB summarizes how much time-weighted desaturation occurs across the night — it reflects both how far SpO₂ drops and how long it stays low around respiratory events. Units are %·min/h (percent–minutes of desaturation “area” per hour of sleep).

When full oximetry waveforms are not available, HB can be approximated from common report lines (ODI, average depth, average duration) or from a list of event depths and durations.

Formulas used here

Direct (triangular approximation per event)
HB = Σ [ ½ × (SpO₂pre − SpO₂nadir) × (duration in min) ] ÷ TST (h)
Sum over scored events; TST = total sleep time in hours.
Indirect (summary-level estimate)
HB ≈ ODI × ½ × Davg × (Tavg / 60)
ODI = desaturations/h; Davg = mean depth (%); Tavg = mean duration (s).

Cardiovascular risk bands (HB)

Illustrative stratification synthesizing published cohort data — not a formal guideline or society-endorsed staging. Must be interpreted with the full sleep study, symptoms, and cardiovascular history.

PALM — pathophysiology map

PALM groups the four classic endotypes that contribute to obstructive sleep apnea severity. It is used to reason about why apnea occurs and which mechanisms may dominate. Full quantification often needs specialized testing; standard polysomnography only provides clues.

P Pharyngeal muscle responsiveness
How effectively dilator muscles stiffen the airway after a challenge. Poor responsiveness → more collapse for a given pressure.
A Anatomy / collapsibility (Pcrit)
Structural predisposition: smaller airway, crowding, obesity-related load — often tracks with severity and positional dependence.
L Arousal threshold
Ease of cortical arousal to respiratory stimuli. A low threshold fragments sleep; a high threshold may allow prolonged obstructive events.
M Loop gain (ventilatory control)
Controller “gain” — high loop gain predisposes to periodic breathing and can interact with obstruction; central events become more relevant.
